# Product

## Register

product

## Users

CECP church members and worship team volunteers use this music library while preparing worship, rehearsing songs, and finding lyrics, scores, keys, tempos, and audio practice tracks. The main context is practical and time-sensitive: before rehearsal, during personal practice, or while preparing a service set.

## Product Purpose

CECP Music Library helps users quickly search worship songs, open lyrics and notation, transpose keys, play audio, manage a small practice queue, and mark songs as favorites or recently used in service. Success means a user can find the right song, confirm the musical details, and begin practicing without hunting through scattered files.

## Brand Personality

Calm, focused, and pastoral. The interface should feel trustworthy and warm, but it must remain a working tool first: clear controls, readable music content, and predictable navigation matter more than decorative polish.

## Anti-references

Avoid entertainment-player excess, heavy visual effects, tiny low-contrast controls, emoji-as-interface decoration, and modal-heavy flows that interrupt rehearsal work. Do not make the app feel like a marketing landing page; users are here to complete a worship preparation task.

## Design Principles

- Make search and song opening feel instant, because finding a song is the primary job.
- Preserve musical readability over visual novelty, especially in notation, lyrics, and transpose controls.
- Keep navigation familiar and stable across desktop, mobile, and installed PWA use.
- Use warm CECP identity quietly, with accent color reserved for action, selection, and state.
- Support real rehearsal conditions: touch-friendly controls, dark mode, reduced motion, and resilient offline or slow-network feedback.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA for text contrast, focus visibility, keyboard operation, and screen reader labels. Preserve reduced-motion behavior, keep tap targets at least 44px where possible, avoid color-only status indicators, and ensure Chinese text remains readable on small screens.
